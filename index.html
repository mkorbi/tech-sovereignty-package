Strengthening Europe's Tech Sovereignty

17 initiatives, one goal: digital independence.

Tech sovereignty is Europe's ability to act independently in the digital world — developing and controlling key technologies, data and infrastructure while reducing reliance on non‑EU providers. Explore how the pieces build on each other, when each arrives, what companies must do, and what it means for you.

Competitiveness Strategic autonomy Fair markets Citizens' rights Innovation
The dependency gap

The EU relies on non‑EU countries for over 80% of key digital products, services, infrastructure and IP. Closing that gap is what this whole agenda is about.

Build capacity

Tens of billions invested in compute, chips, quantum and networks — the things Europe must be able to make itself.

Set fair rules

Laws like the AI Act, DSA and DMA protect rights and keep markets contestable.

Unlock data & scale

Data and start‑up strategies give European firms the fuel and finance to grow at home.

Simplify & secure

Newer moves cut red tape and harden supply chains and connected products.
