# Cyber Resilience Act (CRA)

**Cluster:** Cyber and IT security
**Type:** Regulation (Regulation (EU) 2024/2847)
**Status:** In force (entered into force December 2024); main obligations apply from 2027 (with earlier reporting obligations from 2026)

## Overview

The Cyber Resilience Act introduces mandatory cybersecurity requirements for **products with digital elements** (hardware and software placed on the EU market), ensuring security throughout the product lifecycle and improving overall cyber resilience in the EU. It is the first horizontal EU law setting baseline security rules for connected products, closing gaps where insecure devices and software created risks across the single market.

## Key elements

- **Security by design and by default** — manufacturers must build in cybersecurity from the design stage and across the whole lifecycle.
- **Vulnerability handling** — obligations to manage vulnerabilities, provide security updates, and report actively exploited vulnerabilities and serious incidents (to ENISA / national authorities) within set deadlines.
- **CE marking and conformity assessment** — products must meet essential cybersecurity requirements to be sold in the EU; higher-risk "important" and "critical" product classes face stricter assessment.
- **Transparency for users** — clearer information on security properties and the support period during which updates will be provided.

## Contribution to tech sovereignty

Raises the security baseline of the entire digital product supply chain in the EU, reducing systemic risk and dependency on insecure components, and embedding resilience as a precondition for the connected, AI-enabled infrastructure the broader strategy is building.
