# AI Factories

**Cluster:** Artificial intelligence
**Type:** Investment / infrastructure programme (via EuroHPC Joint Undertaking)
**Page last updated:** 23 April 2026

## Overview

AI Factories leverage the supercomputing capacity of the EuroHPC Joint Undertaking to develop trustworthy, cutting-edge generative AI models. They are ecosystems that bring together computing power, data, and talent, linking supercomputing centres, universities, SMEs, industry, and financial actors, and prioritising access for AI startups and SMEs. They serve sectors such as health, manufacturing, climate, finance, and space.

## Status and scale

- **19 AI Factories and 13 Antennas** operational (antennas are associated with AI-optimised supercomputers in existing factories).
- At least **9 new AI-optimised supercomputers** to be procured and deployed across the EU — more than tripling current EuroHPC AI computing capacity.
- Roll-out waves: 7 consortia selected Dec 2024 (15 Member States + 2 participating states); 6 more in March 2025 (Austria, Bulgaria, France, Germany, Poland, Slovenia); 6 more in October 2025 (Czechia, Lithuania, Netherlands, Romania, Spain, Poland) plus 13 antennas across Belgium, Cyprus, Hungary, Ireland, Latvia, Malta, Slovakia, and partner countries (Iceland, Moldova, North Macedonia, Serbia, Switzerland, UK).

## AI Gigafactories and InvestAI

The **InvestAI Facility** will create a new European fund of **€20 billion** to build up to **5 AI Gigafactories** — large-scale facilities for training next-generation models with trillions of parameters, each bringing together over 100,000 advanced AI processors, with emphasis on power capacity, reliable supply chains, networking, and energy efficiency.

## Funding context

Over 2021–2027, combined Commission, Member State, and Associated Country investment in supercomputing and AI Factories will reach **€10 billion** through the EuroHPC JU.

## Contribution to tech sovereignty

Provides the sovereign compute backbone for European AI, ensuring researchers, startups, and SMEs can train and deploy models in Europe rather than depending on non-EU cloud and compute providers.
