# Data Act

**Cluster:** Data and innovation
**Type:** Regulation (Regulation (EU) 2023/2854)
**Status:** In force (entered into force January 2024; main provisions applicable from September 2025)

## Overview

The Data Act harmonises rules on access to and use of data generated in the EU, ensuring fairness in data sharing and enabling users and businesses to benefit from data generated by connected products and services (the Internet of Things). It is a cornerstone of the European strategy for data, complementing the earlier Data Governance Act.

## Key elements

- **Access for users** — users of connected products (e.g. smart devices, vehicles, machines) and related services get the right to access the data they generate and to share it with third parties.
- **Fair B2B data sharing** — measures to prevent abuse of contractual imbalances and unfair terms imposed on SMEs, including model contractual terms.
- **Cloud switching** — provisions making it easier for customers to switch between cloud and edge providers and to avoid vendor lock-in, including the gradual removal of switching charges.
- **Public-sector access** — rules allowing public bodies to access privately held data in exceptional circumstances (e.g. public emergencies).
- **Interoperability and safeguards** — standards for data spaces and protections against unlawful international governmental access to non-personal data held in the EU.

## Relationship to other initiatives

The Data Act is being further simplified through the **Digital Omnibus**, and supported by guidance (model contractual terms, standard cloud clauses, a legal helpdesk) highlighted in the **Data Union Strategy**.

## Contribution to tech sovereignty

Rebalances control over industrial and IoT data toward European users and businesses, curbs lock-in by dominant (largely non-EU) cloud providers, and protects EU-held data from unjustified foreign access.
