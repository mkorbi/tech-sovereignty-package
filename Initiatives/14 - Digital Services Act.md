# Digital Services Act (DSA)

**Cluster:** A fair, competitive and secure digital environment
**Type:** Regulation (Regulation (EU) 2022/2065)
**Status:** In force; fully applicable to all in-scope services since 17 February 2024 (obligations for very large platforms applied from 2023)

## Overview

The Digital Services Act establishes clear obligations for online intermediaries and platforms to improve transparency, strengthen user protection, and address illegal and harmful content in the EU digital space. Its guiding principle: "what is illegal offline should be illegal online." It modernises the e-Commerce Directive framework for the platform era.

## Key obligations

- **Notice-and-action** mechanisms to report and remove illegal content, with safeguards against over-removal.
- **Transparency** on content moderation, terms of service, and advertising (including bans on targeting ads to minors and on using sensitive personal data for ad targeting).
- **Protection of minors** and user empowerment (e.g. options not to receive recommender systems based on profiling).
- **Tiered, asymmetric rules** — the strictest obligations fall on **Very Large Online Platforms (VLOPs)** and **Very Large Online Search Engines (VLOSEs)** with 45 million+ EU users, including annual systemic-risk assessments, independent audits, and data access for researchers.
- **Enforcement** — the Commission directly supervises VLOPs/VLOSEs; national Digital Services Coordinators oversee others. Fines up to 6% of global annual turnover.

## Contribution to tech sovereignty

Asserts European regulatory control over the largest (mostly non-EU) platforms operating in the EU, protecting citizens' rights and democratic processes online and setting a global benchmark for platform governance.
