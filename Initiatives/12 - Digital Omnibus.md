# Digital Omnibus

**Cluster:** Data and innovation (simplification)
**Type:** Proposed Regulation package (COM(2025) 837)
**Published:** 19 November 2025

## Overview

The Digital Omnibus is a simplification package: a set of technical amendments across a large body of EU digital legislation, selected to bring immediate relief to businesses, public administrations, and citizens, and to stimulate competitiveness. It is described as a first step to "optimise the application of the digital rulebook" — ensuring compliance costs less, still delivers the same objectives, and becomes a competitive advantage for responsible businesses.

## What it does

- **Streamlines and consolidates** existing digital rules to reduce administrative burden and improve coherence across legislation (touching data law including the Data Act, cybersecurity, ePrivacy/electronic communications, eID and trust services, and AI-related rules).
- **Cuts compliance costs** for businesses — especially SMEs — projected to save billions.
- Published **alongside the Data Union Strategy**, and accompanied by measures such as new digital wallets, as part of a broader push to make the EU digital rulebook more agile.

## Context

It responds to criticism (echoed in the Draghi and Letta reports) that the cumulative weight of overlapping EU digital regulation has raised costs and slowed European innovation. It marks a shift in emphasis from rule-making toward rule-simplification.

## Contribution to tech sovereignty

Tackles the "regulatory burden" dimension of competitiveness — reducing friction and duplication so European businesses can spend more on innovation and scaling and less on compliance, without abandoning the underlying protections.
