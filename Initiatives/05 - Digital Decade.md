# The Digital Decade

**Cluster:** The Digital Decade (overarching framework)
**Type:** Policy programme (Digital Decade Policy Programme 2030, Decision (EU) 2022/2481)
**Status:** In force; annual monitoring cycle through 2030

## Overview

The Digital Decade sets the EU's targets for 2030 to strengthen Europe's technological capacity and competitiveness. It is the overarching framework that gives the broader tech sovereignty effort concrete, measurable goals and a governance/monitoring mechanism.

## The four target areas (2030)

1. **Digital skills** — e.g. at least 80% of adults with basic digital skills and 20 million ICT specialists employed in the EU.
2. **Digital infrastructure** — gigabit connectivity for all and 5G everywhere; Europe's share in production of cutting-edge semiconductors; deployment of edge nodes; and progress toward quantum computing.
3. **Digitalisation of business** — high uptake of cloud, big data, and AI among companies; the large majority of SMEs reaching at least a basic level of digital intensity; and growth of European tech "unicorns."
4. **Digitalisation of public services** — 100% online provision of key public services, plus access to e-health records and digital identity.

## Governance

The programme runs a **cooperative annual cycle**: the Commission publishes a *State of the Digital Decade* report tracking progress against targets, Member States set national roadmaps and trajectories, and gaps are addressed through recommendations and multi-country projects.

## Contribution to tech sovereignty

It is the measurement and coordination backbone: it translates "sovereignty" into quantified 2030 targets across infrastructure, skills, business, and public services, and holds Member States and the Commission jointly accountable for closing the gaps.
