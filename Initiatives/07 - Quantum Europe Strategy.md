# Quantum Europe Strategy

**Cluster:** Infrastructure and strategic technologies
**Type:** Commission Communication (COM(2025) 363)
**Adopted:** 2 July 2025

## Overview

The Quantum Europe Strategy aims to position Europe as a global leader in quantum technology by 2030. Despite strong scientific progress, the EU lags in translating research into market opportunities and suffers from fragmented strategies across Member States. The Strategy seeks to turn Europe into a "quantum powerhouse" by building a resilient, sovereign quantum ecosystem that fuels startup growth and moves breakthrough science to market while maintaining scientific leadership.

## Five interconnected areas

1. **Research and Innovation** — consolidating excellence to lead in quantum science and its industrial transformation.
2. **Quantum Infrastructures** — scalable, coordinated infrastructure hubs for production, design, and application development.
3. **Strengthening the Quantum Ecosystem** — investment in startups and scaleups, securing supply chains, industrialisation.
4. **Space and Dual-Use Quantum Technologies** — integrating secure, sovereign quantum capabilities into Europe's space, security, and defence strategies.
5. **Quantum Skills** — building a world-class workforce via coordinated education, training, and talent mobility.

## Contribution to tech sovereignty

Targets a strategically critical next-generation technology — including dual-use and security-relevant applications — where early sovereign capacity and a unified European approach are seen as essential to avoid future dependency.
