# EU Open Source Strategy

**Cluster:** Data and innovation / openness (horizontal)
**Type:** Commission Strategy — part of the European Technological Sovereignty Package (Communication COM(2026) 503)
**Status:** Adopted 3 June 2026

## Overview

The EU Open Source Strategy places open source at the centre of Europe's technological sovereignty. It promotes European open alternatives to non-EU proprietary solutions in critical domains, and aims to strengthen Europe's open digital ecosystems by supporting the development, scaling, deployment and long-term sustainability of open-source technologies across both the public and private sectors. It was adopted alongside the Communication on European Tech Sovereignty, in the same package as the Cloud and AI Development Act (CADA), the Chips Act 2.0, and the Strategic Roadmap for Digitalisation and AI in Energy.

## Three pillars

The strategy is built around three mutually reinforcing pillars:

**Trusted assets** — a resilient open digital ecosystem depends on secure, well-maintained building blocks such as shared code repositories and flexible licensing schemes.

**Empowered communities** — supporting the open-source communities, maintainers and contributors that keep critical software healthy over time.

**Strong governance** — governance structures that ensure long-term sustainability, security and coordination of open-source efforts.

## What it means for companies

Businesses and software vendors can build, scale and sustain European open-source alternatives, with support aimed at the long-term maintenance and funding that open projects often lack. Companies and public bodies gain access to trusted, shared assets (repositories, licensing schemes) they can reuse rather than rebuild, and public administrations are steered toward open source in procurement — reinforced by CADA's open-source-first approach and the EU Open-Source Solutions Catalogue.

## What it means for end users

Less lock-in to proprietary, often non-EU software; more transparent and auditable technology behind public services; and more resilient, sustainably maintained digital systems — all contributing to European digital autonomy across the whole technology stack.

## How it supports tech sovereignty

Open source is the software-layer lever against the EU's 80%+ dependency on non-EU technology. By making open, reusable, well-governed software the default across the stack, the strategy reduces reliance on proprietary foreign solutions, keeps capability and control in Europe, and underpins the open-source commitments embedded in CADA, the Apply AI Strategy ("buy European / open source") and the EU Open-Source Solutions Catalogue.

## Relationship to the OSS Catalogue

The EU Open Source Strategy is the **policy umbrella**; the **EU Open-Source Solutions Catalogue** (file 13) is one of the concrete tools that serves it, giving public administrations a place to discover, share and reuse the open-source assets the strategy promotes.
