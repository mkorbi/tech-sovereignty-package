# Digital Markets Act (DMA)

**Cluster:** A fair, competitive and secure digital environment
**Type:** Regulation (Regulation (EU) 2022/1925)
**Status:** In force; applicable since May 2023, with gatekeeper compliance obligations from March 2024

## Overview

The Digital Markets Act introduces rules for large digital platforms designated as **"gatekeepers"** to ensure fair competition, prevent unfair practices, and improve contestability in digital markets. It targets structural market power that competition law alone was slow to address, setting clear ex-ante "do's and don'ts."

## How it works

- **Gatekeeper designation** — applies to large platforms providing "core platform services" (e.g. app stores, search engines, browsers, operating systems, messaging, social networks, online intermediation) that meet size, user, and entrenchment thresholds.
- **Obligations ("do's")** — e.g. allow interoperability, let business users access the data they generate, permit promotion of offers outside the platform, and allow uninstalling pre-installed apps and using alternative app stores.
- **Prohibitions ("don'ts")** — e.g. no self-preferencing of the gatekeeper's own products in rankings, no combining personal data across services without consent, no preventing users from reaching businesses they met on the platform.
- **Enforcement** — supervised by the Commission, with fines up to 10% of global turnover (20% for repeat infringements) and possible structural remedies.

## Contribution to tech sovereignty

Rebalances power between dominant (mostly non-EU) gatekeepers and European businesses and consumers, opening space for European competitors and giving the EU direct leverage over how the largest platforms operate in its market.
