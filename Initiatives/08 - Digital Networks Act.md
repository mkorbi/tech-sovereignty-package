# Digital Networks Act (DNA)

**Cluster:** Infrastructure and strategic technologies
**Type:** Proposed Regulation
**Status:** Proposal adopted by the Commission on 21 January 2026

## Overview

The Digital Networks Act will modernise and simplify the EU legal framework for connectivity to boost investment in advanced, resilient digital infrastructure (high-capacity fibre, 5G, and future networks). It builds on the 2018 EU Electronic Communications Code (EECC) and responds to the Draghi, Letta, and Niinistö reports, which found Europe's telecoms market fragmented into 27 national markets, limiting operators' ability to invest, scale, and compete globally.

## Consolidation

The DNA merges four legal acts into one directly applicable Regulation, replacing the **European Electronic Communications Code (EECC)**, the **BEREC Regulation**, the **Radio Spectrum Policy Programme**, and core parts of the **Open Internet Regulation**.

## Key measures

- **Single Market for connectivity** — a "Single Passport" authorisation (notification in only one Member State) and EU-level satellite spectrum authorisation to enable pan-European operations.
- **Simplification** — reduced regulatory and administrative burden, more flexibility for B2B relations, while keeping high consumer protection.
- **Innovation in the digital ecosystem** — clearer Open Internet rules for innovative services and a voluntary cooperation mechanism on IP interconnection and traffic efficiency.
- **Resilience and preparedness** — an EU-level Preparedness Plan against natural disasters and foreign interference, plus security/resilience criteria for pan-EU satellite communications.
- **Investment-friendly spectrum** — longer licence durations, renewals, and spectrum sharing.
- **Copper-to-fibre transition** — mandatory national transition plans for the sustainable phase-out of copper networks.

## Contribution to tech sovereignty

Aims to give Europe a unified, investable connectivity market and resilient networks — the physical foundation for AI, cloud, and space — while reducing dependencies (e.g. in satellite communications) and strengthening crisis preparedness.
