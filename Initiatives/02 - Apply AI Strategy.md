# Apply AI Strategy

**Cluster:** Artificial intelligence
**Type:** Commission Strategy (COM(2025) 723) — key deliverable of the AI Continent Action Plan
**Presented:** October 2025 (page last updated 3 June 2026)

## Overview

The Apply AI Strategy is the EU's overarching AI sectoral strategy, designed to boost AI adoption and innovation across Europe — especially among SMEs — and to strengthen the EU's technological sovereignty. It promotes an **"AI-first" policy** (AI considered as a potential solution in strategic and policy decisions, weighing benefits and risks) and a **"buy European" approach** for the public sector, with a focus on open-source AI.

## Three sections

1. **Sectoral flagships** — targeted measures to boost AI adoption across 10 key industry sectors plus the public sector. Sectors include healthcare and pharmaceuticals; mobility/transport/automotive; robotics; manufacturing/engineering/construction; climate and environment; energy; agri-food; defence, security and space; electronic communications; and cultural, creative and media. Example: AI-powered advanced screening centres in healthcare.
2. **Cross-cutting support to raise tech sovereignty** — reinforces the European Digital Innovation Hubs (transformed into **Experience Centres for AI**), connects to AI Factories/Gigafactories, AI Testing and Experimentation Facilities, and AI regulatory sandboxes, plus measures for an AI-ready workforce.
3. **New governance** — the **Apply AI Alliance** (coordination forum of providers, industry, academia, public sector) and an **AI Observatory** to track trends and impact.

## Complementary strands

Presented alongside the **AI in Science Strategy** (with the RAISE pilot — Resource for AI Science in Europe). Complemented by the **Data Union Strategy** (data availability) and the proposed **Cloud and AI Development Act (CADA)**, both part of the tech sovereignty package, plus a roadmap for digitalisation and AI in the energy sector.

## Contribution to tech sovereignty

Directly targets adoption — the area where Europe most lags — while steering demand toward European and open-source AI solutions and building sovereign, secure models trained on European data.
