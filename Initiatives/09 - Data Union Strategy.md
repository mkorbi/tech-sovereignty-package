# Data Union Strategy

**Cluster:** Data and innovation
**Type:** Commission Communication (COM(2025) 835)
**Published:** December 2025 (alongside the Digital Omnibus)

## Overview

The Data Union Strategy seeks to increase the availability of high-quality data for AI development, simplify EU data rules, and strengthen the EU's global position on international data flows. Its premise: to compete in an AI-shaped global economy, Europe must be able to fuel AI with high-quality data, exploit untapped data potential, and complete the Single Market for data. Benefits target SMEs (cheaper, simpler data access), researchers (high-quality training data), and consumers (more innovation and services).

## Three priority areas

1. **Scaling up access to data for AI** — launch the first **data labs** (pooling private and public resources to make sectoral data available); scale up common European **data spaces** (~€100 million ongoing EU investment, including a defence data space); expand high-value datasets under the Open Data Directive; make **30 million digitised cultural objects** available for AI training; and boost synthetic data.
2. **Streamlining data rules** — published alongside the **Digital Omnibus**, which modernises and consolidates EU data laws (including the Data Act). Supporting tools: model contractual terms for data access, standard contractual clauses for cloud contracts, and a Data Act legal helpdesk.
3. **Safeguarding EU data sovereignty** — a strategic approach to international data flows combining openness with strength: guidelines to assess fair treatment of EU data abroad, and a toolbox to counter unjustified localisation, data leakage, and to protect sensitive non-personal data.

## Contribution to tech sovereignty

Directly addresses the "data" pillar of AI competitiveness — ensuring European businesses have the high-quality data needed to build AI domestically — while defending control over how European data flows internationally.
