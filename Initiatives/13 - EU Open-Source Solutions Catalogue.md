# EU Open-Source Solutions Catalogue (EU OSS Catalogue)

**Cluster:** Data and innovation
**Type:** Platform / tool (managed via Interoperable Europe)
**Status:** Operational

## Overview

The EU Open-Source Solutions Catalogue is a central platform for public administrations to discover, share, and reuse open-source software. It helps public bodies across the EU find existing solutions rather than building or procuring duplicative software, supporting interoperability and reuse across borders and administrative levels.

## Key features

- A **single discovery point** for open-source tools developed and used by public administrations.
- Supports **sharing and reuse** of solutions between Member States and institutions, reducing cost and duplication.
- Part of the **Interoperable Europe** framework promoting cross-border digital public services.
- Aligns with the broader EU push toward open source in the public sector (including the "buy European" and open-source emphasis of the Apply AI Strategy and the EU Open Source Strategy).

## Context

It connects to wider sovereignty efforts such as the Digital Commons EDIC (launched December 2025) and the call for evidence on Open-Source Digital Ecosystems (January 2026), reflecting open source as a lever for reducing dependency on proprietary, often non-EU, software.

## Contribution to tech sovereignty

Open source reduces vendor lock-in and dependency on proprietary non-EU software, gives public administrations control over the technology they run, and keeps value and capability within Europe through shared, reusable code.
