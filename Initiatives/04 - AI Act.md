# AI Act

**Cluster:** Artificial intelligence
**Type:** Regulation (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689) — the first comprehensive legal framework on AI
**Status:** In force (entered into force 1 August 2024; obligations phasing in through 2026–2027)

## Overview

The AI Act is the world's first comprehensive legal framework on artificial intelligence. It aims to foster innovative and trustworthy AI in Europe while protecting health, safety, and fundamental rights, positioning the EU to play a leading global role in trustworthy AI.

## Risk-based approach

The Act regulates AI according to the level of risk a system poses:

- **Unacceptable risk** — prohibited practices (e.g. social scoring, certain manipulative or exploitative systems).
- **High risk** — systems in sensitive areas (e.g. critical infrastructure, education, employment, essential services, law enforcement) subject to strict requirements on risk management, data governance, transparency, human oversight, and conformity assessment.
- **Limited risk** — transparency obligations (e.g. disclosing AI interaction, labelling synthetic content).
- **Minimal risk** — largely unregulated.

It also sets specific rules for **general-purpose AI (GPAI) models**, including transparency and, for the most capable models, systemic-risk obligations.

## Governance

Implementation is supported by the **European AI Office** within the Commission, alongside national authorities. The Act introduces phased application dates, with prohibitions, GPAI rules, and high-risk obligations entering into effect at staggered intervals after entry into force.

## Contribution to tech sovereignty

By setting a single, rights-protective rulebook for AI across the EU, the Act seeks to build trust, create a harmonised internal market, and let Europe shape global AI norms — a regulatory complement to the capacity-building of the AI Continent Action Plan, AI Factories, and Apply AI Strategy.
