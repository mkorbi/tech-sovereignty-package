# ICT Supply Chain Security Toolbox

**Cluster:** Cyber and IT security
**Type:** Coordinated EU approach / policy toolbox
**Status:** Active

## Overview

The ICT Supply Chain Security Toolbox provides a coordinated EU approach to identify, assess, and mitigate cybersecurity risks in ICT supply chains. It supports Member States in reducing strategic dependencies and strengthening security across the information and communications technology supply chain. It follows the model of the earlier 5G Cybersecurity Toolbox, extending that risk-based, coordinated method to ICT supply chains more broadly.

## Key elements

- **Risk identification and assessment** — a common framework for mapping and evaluating cybersecurity risks across ICT supply chains, including risks from high-risk vendors and concentration/dependency.
- **Mitigation measures** — a menu of strategic and technical measures Member States can apply to reduce exposure and diversify suppliers.
- **Coordination** — a shared EU approach so Member States act consistently rather than in fragmented national silos, complementing instruments such as the NIS2 Directive and the Cyber Resilience Act.

## Contribution to tech sovereignty

Directly targets dependency risk in critical ICT supply chains — the channel through which strategic and security vulnerabilities most often enter — helping Member States reduce reliance on high-risk or single-source non-EU suppliers.
