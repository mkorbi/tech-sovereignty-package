# European Chips Act

**Cluster:** Infrastructure and strategic technologies
**Type:** Regulation (Regulation (EU) 2023/1781)
**Status:** In force since 21 September 2023 (review / "Chips Act 2.0" under discussion)

## Why it exists

Chips (semiconductors) are the building block of all electronic products and underpin the digital transformation across cars, communications, data processing, space, defence, and smart devices. The global chip shortage exposed Europe's supply-chain vulnerability. The Act reinforces the EU semiconductor ecosystem, ensures supply-chain resilience, reduces external dependencies, and supports the Digital Decade target of **doubling the EU's global semiconductor market share to 20% by 2030**.

## Five strategic objectives

1. Strengthening research and technological leadership.
2. Building Europe's capacity to innovate in design, manufacturing, and packaging of advanced chips.
3. Putting in place a framework to increase production by 2030.
4. Addressing the skills shortage and attracting talent.
5. Developing deep understanding of the global semiconductor supply chain.

## Three pillars

- **Pillar I — "Chips for Europe" Initiative:** large-scale capacity building via the Chips Joint Undertaking, including five pilot lines (lab-to-fab) and competence centres now established in all Member States plus Norway.
- **Pillar II — Security of supply and resilience:** a framework attracting manufacturing investment. The Commission has approved **seven (now more) State aid decisions** on first-of-a-kind facilities totalling over **€31.5 billion** in public and private investment (companies include STMicroelectronics, GlobalFoundries, ESMC/TSMC in Dresden, Infineon, Silicon Box, ams Osram, onsemi, X-Fab). Includes "integrated production facility" and "open EU foundry" status.
- **Pillar III — Monitoring and crisis response:** the **European Semiconductor Board** coordinates Member States and the Commission to monitor the value chain and respond to crises.

## Recent developments

In September 2025 the **Semicon Coalition** (all 27 Member States) signed a Declaration calling for a **Chips Act 2.0**, and the Commission opened a public consultation/review. Recent milestones include the €700 million NanoIC pilot line (Feb 2026) and continued State aid approvals into 2026.

## Contribution to tech sovereignty

The flagship hardware-sovereignty instrument: it reduces strategic dependency in the most foundational technology layer by rebuilding European design, manufacturing, and packaging capacity.
