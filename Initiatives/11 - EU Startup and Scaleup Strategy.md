# EU Startup and Scaleup Strategy

**Cluster:** Data and innovation
**Type:** Commission Strategy (led by DG Research and Innovation)
**Status:** Adopted 2025

## Overview

The EU Startup and Scaleup Strategy supports innovative companies in Europe by improving access to finance, reducing regulatory barriers, and strengthening conditions for growth and cross-border scaling in the Single Market. It addresses a long-standing European weakness: strong research and early-stage innovation, but difficulty scaling companies to global size (the gap analysed in the Draghi and Letta reports).

## Focus areas

- **Access to finance** — mobilising more late-stage and scale-up capital (venture capital, institutional investment) so European startups need not relocate abroad to raise growth funding.
- **Reducing regulatory and administrative barriers** — simpler rules and a more favourable environment for founders, including work toward a more harmonised "28th regime"-style framework for innovative companies operating across borders.
- **Cross-border scaling** — easing expansion across the Single Market, harmonising conditions, and reducing fragmentation between national markets.
- **Talent and ecosystem support** — improving access to talent, stock-options regimes, and links to research, compute, and data infrastructure.

## Contribution to tech sovereignty

Aims to keep high-growth European tech companies — including in AI, deep tech, and quantum — headquartered, funded, and scaling in Europe, reducing the loss of strategic firms and IP to non-EU markets.
